XES LL spectrum [1]
  In fig. 4 the XES LL, spectrum arising from the 2p+3/2 -> 3s+ transition, is contrasted with photoelectron spectra representing both the initial and final states in the electron rearrangement, i.e., the 2p3/2 and 3s spectra. Whereas multiplet splitting in the final 3s+ state will correspond to low energy satellites in the XES spectra, similar effects in the initial 2p+ state should give rise to high energy satellites in the LL, spectrum. L spectra of transition metal atoms are complicated by Coster-Kronig Auger events within the L shell, particularly the LII LIII MIV,V processes [20]. This reduces the intensity of the Ln(2p1/2 -> 3s) and results in LL emissions from doubly ionised atoms. Double ionisation satellites also result after LI LII, IIIM Auger events.
[1] P.R. Wood and D.S. Urch, Chem.Phys. Letters, 37 (1976) 13.
